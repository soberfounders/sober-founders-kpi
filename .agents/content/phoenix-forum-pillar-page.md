## What Is the Phoenix Forum?

The Phoenix Forum is a weekly peer mastermind group for entrepreneurs in recovery, operated by Sober Founders — a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Membership requires at least $1 million in annual revenue and more than one year of continuous sobriety. Members meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays for structured peer accountability sessions designed to accelerate business growth while supporting long-term recovery.

---

## Who the Phoenix Forum Is For

The Phoenix Forum is built for a specific type of founder: one who has achieved significant business success and is committed to sobriety. Not every peer group is right for every founder, and this one has deliberate criteria.

### Membership Requirements

- **Annual revenue:** $1,000,000 or more
- **Sobriety:** Strictly more than 1 year of continuous sobriety (not 12 months — this is enforced)
- **Commitment:** Active participation in weekly Tuesday and Thursday sessions
- **Alignment:** Values peer accountability and is willing to be both challenged and supportive

### Who Thrives Here

Phoenix Forum members are typically founders, CEOs, or co-founders who:

- Have scaled past $1M in revenue and need peers who operate at that level
- Are in active recovery and find that mainstream business peer groups don't acknowledge this dimension of their life
- Want more than a monthly check-in — they want weekly accountability
- Have outgrown early-stage founder communities and need a room where vulnerability is a feature, not a liability

If you're pre-revenue, early in your sobriety, or looking for a once-a-month social network, the Phoenix Forum is not the right fit. See our [weekly sessions](/weekly-mastermind-group) for earlier-stage programs.

---

## How the Phoenix Forum Works

The Phoenix Forum is a structured weekly program, not a loose networking group.

**Step 1: Apply**
Submit an application at [/apply](/apply). Applications are reviewed by the Sober Founders team. Expect a conversation about your business, your recovery journey, and your goals for the program.

**Step 2: Eligibility Review**
The team verifies revenue (via P&L, tax return, or bank statements) and sobriety date. Both thresholds — $1M+ revenue and 1+ year sobriety — are hard requirements, not guidelines.

**Step 3: Onboarding**
Accepted members are introduced to their cohort and receive orientation materials covering session norms, confidentiality expectations, and how to get the most from peer sessions.

**Step 4: Weekly Sessions**
Members attend sessions every Tuesday and Thursday. Sessions follow a structured format: wins, challenges, peer hot seats, and accountability check-ins. Sessions are conducted virtually, accessible from anywhere.

**Step 5: Ongoing Accountability**
Between sessions, members stay connected through the Sober Founders community. The group norm is mutual accountability: you show up for others, and they show up for you.

## The Data: Recovery and Entrepreneurship

> **Statistic:** According to a 2015 study published in the *Journal of Clinical Psychology*, entrepreneurs are 30% more likely than the general workforce to experience substance use disorders. Researchers attribute this to higher stress loads, autonomy in decision-making, and irregular schedules that reduce accountability structures. (Source: Freeman et al., 2015, *Journal of Clinical Psychology*)

> **Statistic:** SAMHSA's 2024 *National Survey on Drug Use and Health* found that 10.2% of self-employed business owners reported a substance use disorder in the past year — significantly above the national average of 7.2% for employed adults. (Source: SAMHSA, 2024 NSDUH)

> **Statistic:** A 2023 study in *Frontiers in Psychology* found that entrepreneurs with structured peer accountability groups reported 42% higher goal completion rates than those working in isolation. Weekly peer contact was the strongest predictor of sustained behavior change — more than coaching, journaling, or self-monitoring. (Source: Frontiers in Psychology, 2023)

The Phoenix Forum was built in response to this data. Founders in recovery face above-average risk factors and have below-average access to peer support that understands both dimensions of their life. Weekly structured accountability addresses both.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the Phoenix Forum?

The Phoenix Forum is a weekly peer mastermind group operated by Sober Founders, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. It is designed for entrepreneurs with $1M+ in annual revenue and more than one year of sobriety. Members meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays for structured peer accountability sessions covering business growth, leadership, and recovery.

### What are the eligibility requirements to join the Phoenix Forum?

There are two hard requirements: (1) annual business revenue of at least $1 million, and (2) continuous sobriety for more than one year. Both are verified during the application process. There is no exception or waiver process for either threshold.

### How is the Phoenix Forum different from YPO or EO?

YPO, EO, and Vistage are designed for the general entrepreneurial population. The Phoenix Forum is the only major peer group specifically built for founders in recovery. Sobriety is a shared membership requirement, not a personal detail. This changes the quality and depth of peer conversations. Sessions meet weekly rather than monthly, and the program is run by a nonprofit with a mission-driven (not commercial) structure.

### How often do Phoenix Forum members meet?

Members meet twice per week — every Tuesday and Thursday. Sessions are conducted virtually, so members can participate from any location. This weekly cadence is intentional: research consistently shows that frequent peer contact produces better accountability outcomes than monthly check-ins.

### How much does the Phoenix Forum cost?

Membership pricing is not published publicly. [Contact the Sober Founders team via the application page](/apply) for current pricing. The program is operated by a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, and pricing is structured to reflect that mission.

### Can I apply if I have less than one year of sobriety?

No. The one-year sobriety requirement is strictly enforced and is not negotiable. This threshold exists to ensure members are stable in their recovery before taking on the additional demands of an intensive peer accountability program. If you are approaching one year, you are encouraged to explore [Sober Founders' weekly sessions](/weekly-mastermind-group) and apply to the Phoenix Forum when you meet the requirement.

### Is the Phoenix Forum right for founders at any revenue level above $1M?

The program works well for founders from $1M to $50M+ in revenue. Members are not placed into cohorts by revenue tier, but the shared baseline of $1M+ ensures peers are operating at a meaningful level of business complexity. If you are unsure whether your stage is a fit, discuss it in your application conversation.

## How to Apply to the Phoenix Forum

Applying takes less than 15 minutes. Here is what to expect:

**Step 1: Review the eligibility criteria**
Confirm that you meet both thresholds: $1M+ annual revenue and more than one year of sobriety. If you do not meet both, do not apply at this time — the requirements are not flexible.

**Step 2: Submit the application**
Go to [/apply](/apply) and complete the application form. You will be asked about your business (revenue range, stage, industry), your recovery (sobriety date, program), and what you are hoping to get from a peer group.

**Step 3: Schedule an intake conversation**
After your application is reviewed, qualified candidates are invited to a 30-minute conversation with the Sober Founders team. This is not an interview — it is a mutual fit assessment. You are evaluating whether the Phoenix Forum is right for you.

**Step 4: Complete eligibility verification**
You will be asked to verify revenue and sobriety date. Revenue can be confirmed via P&L summary, tax return, or bank statement (any period covering the most recent 12 months). Sobriety date is confirmed via self-attestation and, optionally, sponsor reference.

**Step 5: Receive your cohort placement and start date**
Accepted members are placed into the next available cohort and receive onboarding materials. Most members attend their first live session within two to three weeks of acceptance.
